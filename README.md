# World-Cup-Qual.-UEFA-Playoffs--Match-Analysis-Turkey-vs-Romania

Match Analysis: Turkey vs Romania
## Objective

To evaluate team performance using shot data, focusing on attacking efficiency, shot quality, and tactical patterns in a competitive international fixture.

## Data Used
Shot-level event data from the match
Key variables:
Shot type (goal, miss, block, save, post)
Situation (assisted, corner, free-kick, regular play)
Player involvement
Shot coordinates (x, y)
Body part used
## Key Findings
1. Turkey Dominated Shot Volume
Turkey (Home): 16 shots
Romania (Away): 6 shots

Turkey generated 73% of total shots, indicating sustained attacking pressure and territorial control.

2. Poor Conversion Despite Control
Turkey:
1 goal from 16 shots (6.25%)
9 missed shots
Romania:
0 goals, 0 shots on target

Despite dominance, Turkey struggled to convert chances — suggesting low shot quality or poor finishing decisions.

3. Centralized Attack Through Key Players
Hakan Çalhanoğlu → 6 shots
Arda Güler → 4 shots

Nearly 60% of Turkey’s shots came from two players, indicating a predictable attacking structure.

4. Romania’s Attacks Were Disrupted Early
Romania:
3 out of 6 shots blocked (50%)

This suggests Turkey’s defensive structure forced shots under pressure, reducing shot quality.

5. Shot Context Matters
Turkey created chances mainly from:
Assisted play
Set pieces (corners, free kicks)
Romania:
Limited open-play penetration
Relied on isolated moments
## Visual Insight

Your shot map shows:

Turkey’s shots clustered in attacking zones

## This visually reinforces:
“High volume ≠ high quality”

⚙️ Tactical Implications
🇹🇷 Turkey
Improve shot selection
Reduce speculative efforts
Diversify attacking threats
Avoid over-reliance on key players
Increase variation in finishing
Use different angles, body parts

The data suggests Turkey’s issue is not chance creation — it is chance efficiency.

🇷🇴 Romania
Improve build-up play
Progress the ball into better shooting zones
Reduce blocked shots
Create clearer shooting lanes
Increase attacking support
Avoid isolated attackers

Romania’s main problem is chance creation, not finishing.

## Final Summary

Turkey controlled the game through shot volume and territorial dominance but lacked efficiency in front of goal.
Romania, meanwhile, struggled to create meaningful chances due to limited attacking structure and strong defensive pressure from Turkey.
